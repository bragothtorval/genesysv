About

The Genomic Data Warehouse (GDW), built-on top of Elasticsearch, provides a database for efficiently storing and retrieving sequencing and variant data. It features tools for accurately selecting disease associated variants and for guiding variant/candidate gene selection process and provide assessment on how likely the selected variants/genes are involved in various genetic diseases.

Currently, the GDW hosts data from clinical sequencing projects initiated by the core facilities located at the Center of Excellence in Bioinformatics & Life Sciences. The database currently contains genomic variant data collected at the University at Buffalo and it's collaborators for the following studies

  • Statin Induced Myopathy

Variants are called using a GATK (v3.5.0) pipeline developed in house.

Data under this release (V2) are based on hg19/GRCh37 coordinates. A demo dataset, which is a subset of somatic mutation data from brain tumors (GBM) downloaded from the Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA) data portal, is provided for users to explore the functionality of this database. To protect the privacy of patient data, all other datasets are under controlled access. If you are interested in mining this database in your research work, please contact our Data Access Office (big-help@buffalo.edu) to inquire about access.
