Use Case 1

Use Case 1: Retrieve variants from the Brain tumor (GBM from TCGA) study that are predicted to be pathogenic by the SIFT and Polyphen2 algorithms, with MAF <= 0.001 in all of the populations from ExAC. Return variant names, genomic coordinates, genes affected, samples that harbor these variants and genotypes for these variants. Save results to a tab-delimited text file.

To begin searching, click the green "Start Searching" button. You will be forwarded to a page that asks you to select study and dataset with two drop-down menus.
Select "Brain tumor (GBM, from TCGA, demo only)" and "Somatic Mutations" for study and dataset, respectively. Click the "Next" button to proceed to the filter selection page.
You will be presented with the "Select Filters" page.
Click the "Variant related information" text in the blue panel heading to collapse this section because we will not be choosing any filters from it.
Click the "Pathogenic predictions" text in the blue panel heading to expand this section. Select "D" for "SIFT pred". Next select both "D" and "P" for "Polyphen2 HDIV pred" by shift-clicking in the multi-select box. If you are not sure about the meanings of the abbreviations used for the filters, you can hover-over the information icon to get detailed information.
Click the "Minor allele frequencies" text in the blue panel heading to expand this section. By default the following filters are already selected: "ExAC All", "G1K All", and "ESP6500 All". Remove these filters by clicking the "x" symbols in the summary panel on the the right hand side of screen. Then the enter value "0.001" for all the "ExAC" filters except "ExAC All". The selected filters also show up in the right panel. Click the "Next" button to proceed to the attribute selection page.
In the attribute selection page, you can select the attributes that you want to include in the search result. Check the attribute names "Variant name", "Chromosome", "Start position", "Ref allele", and "Alt allele" under the "Variant related information" section. Then click the "Variant related information" text in the blue panel heading to collapse it.
Expand the "Gene/Transcript" section and select "Symbol" and "RefGene ID"under the "NCBI RefSeq IDs" sub-section. Click the panel heading to collapse it.
Click the "Patient/Sample Related Information" panel title text to expand it. Check "Patient/Sample ID and "Genotype". Click the green "Search" button to begin searching.
You can sort the results by columns and also filter the results by searching. By default, up to 1000 search results are shown as a preview. You can examine the results and decide if you need to modify your search criteria. You can use the "Back" button to go back to the attribute and/or filter pages and change the parameters and then click the "Search" button to update the search results. To download all results that match your criteria, you can click the "Download" button at the top-right corner to save the search result as a tab-delimited text file.

Use Case 2

Use Case 2: A researcher is interested in studying the "NFIX" gene, which has been shown to affect muscle development. They are interested in identifying non-silent variants in "NFIX" as well as other genes from the nuclear factor 1 family, such as "NFIA", "NFIB" and "NFIC" genes from the samples in the Brain tumor (GBM from TCGA) dataset.

Begin searching, by selecting the "Brain tumor (GBM, from TCGA, demo only)" study and the "Somatic Mutations" dataset.
In the filter selection page, expand the Genes/Transcript section and under the "NCBI refSeq identifier" sub-section, specify symbols: "NFIX", "NFIA", "NFIB", and "NFIC". Also remove the default filters: "ExAC All (<): 0.001", "G1K All (<): 0.001" and "ESP6500(<): 0.001" by clicking the "x" symbols next to them inside the summary panel on the right hand side under the "Filters section.
Expand the Functional consequences section and under the "Consequence(s) to RefSeq Gene/Transcript(s)" sub-section, select the following choices: "nonsynonymous_SNV", "splicing", "stopgain" and "stoploss". You can select multiple choices by holding down the shiftkey and making the selections.
Expand the "Minor allele frequencies" section and under the "The Exome Aggregation Consortium (ExAC)" sub-section, input "0.05" for "ExAC All available populations (<)". Click the "Next" button to proceed to the attribute selection page.
From the "Variant Related Information" section select the following attributes: "Chromosome", "Start position", "Ref allele", "Alt allele" and "dbSNP ID". Collapse the current section by clicking the text in blue panel heading.
Expand the "Functional Consequences". Select "Consequence to Ensembl gene/transcript(s)" attribute. Collapse the current section by clicking its title text in blue panel heading.
Expand the "Gene/Transcript" section. From the "NCBI RefSeq IDs" sub-section, select "Symbol" attribute. Collapse the current section by clicking its title text in blue panel heading.
Expand the "Patient/Sample Related Information" section. Select the attributes "Patient ID" and "Genotype". Click the green "Search" button to begin searching.
Results show that the demo dataset only has one match.
